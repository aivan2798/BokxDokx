He   rode   the   elevator   down   to   his   level,   fumbling   in   his   pocket   for   the
Freeside  credit  chip  that  served  as  his  key.  Sleep  was  becoming  real,  was
something  he  might  do.  To  lie  down  on  the  sand-colored  temperfoam  and
find the blankness again.

They were waiting there, the three of them, their perfect white sportsclothes
and  stenciled  tans  setting  off  the  handwoven  organic  chic  of  the  furniture.
The  girl  sat  on  a  wicker  sofa,  an  automatic  pistol  beside  her  on  the  leaf-
patterned print of the cushion.

"Turing," she said. "You are under arrest."

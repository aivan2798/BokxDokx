"Well,"  she  said,  pausing,  taking  all  of  her  weight  on  her  right  leg,  "guess
you got problems too." She looked down. There was a faint circle of light,
no  larger  than  the  brass  round  of  the  Chubb  key  that  dangled  between  her
breasts. She looked up. Nothing at all. She tongued her amps and the tube
rose   into   vanishing   perspective,   the   Braun   picking   its   way   up   the   rungs.
"Nobody told me about this part," she said.
